Mapathon

Definition:

A mapathon is a coordinated mapping event. It is often held inside (armchair mapping) but can also be an outside or combined.[1]

Objectives:

To improve the quality, accuracy and content of open mapping files for the primary benefit of humanitarian causes.

Audience:

Mapping and citizen science communities / all welcome

Method:

Armchair mapping - tracing maps from Bing and other satellite imaging sources. Translate hard copy maps to online.

Outside mapping - working in the field to capture mapping information online

Photography or cartography / citizen science

Topic: 

Geographic area and or specific humanitarian disaster/ geographic change

Mapping integrity

Time:

1 hour – 1 day

Outputs:

  • New maps
  • Community/new relationships

Outcomes:

  • Increased awareness of a specific issue/local area

Case Studies

1. Missing Maps Project, Medecins sans Frontieres (MSF), global

Format: Open collaboration encouraging NGOs to run their own mapping parties and/or contribute data for specific ‘missing map’ areas.

Topic: Map the places in the world where the most vulnerable people live preemptively

Incentive: Contribution to a social/humanitarian cause, sense of achievement

Goal: Map the most vulnerable places in the developing world, in order that international and local NGOs, and individuals can use the maps and data to better respond to crises affecting the areas. And to support OpenStreetMap, and specifically the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT), in developing technologies, skills, workflows and communities in order to achieve this.

Further information: http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Missing_Maps_Project 

 

2. Night of the living Maps, Global Mapathon, 2012

Format: Mappers come together in local meetings and trace imagery the whole night long, while the moon shines bright

Topic: Organisers of local mapathons are invited to set their own target area

Incentive: Contribution to a citizen science project

Goal: To boost mapping completeness in countryside areas by tracing Bing maps

3. Operation Cowboy, Global Mapathon, 2012

Format: 24hr mapping parties across the globe, snacks and drinks funded centrally

Topic: Make simple improvements to data in the U.S.

Incentive: Contribution to a citizen science project

Goal: To make simple improvements to mapping data in the U.S. Explicitly a training event to help more people learn to map.

This is a an "armchair mapping" project, meaning we are seeking to make simple improvements using aerial imagery, and refining the low quality imported data.

4. Peace Corps Mapathon, Peace Corps, USAID, and Centers for Disease Control, 2016

Format: 2 hour event open to the public

Topic: Methods to decrease the impact of malaria

Incentive: Contribution to a citizen science project

Goal: Edit OpenStreetMap, and contribute to data used to prevent and control malaria.

5. AAG Mapathon, MapGive (an initiative of the U.S. Department of State's Humanitarian Information Unit), George Mason University, and friends, San Francisco, March 2016

Format: Three day Mapathon during AAG (American Association of Geographers) conference with complex schedule of specific projects, talks and mapathons

Topic: Three specific mapping projects available - e.g. Ethiopia - Mekelle and Nepal - Pokhara

Incentive: Contribution to a citizen science project, be a good global geoinformation steward, get inspired by interesting talks and daily keynote lectures

Goal: Contribute to OpenStreetMap for humanitarian efforts, networking with industry leaders and academic researchers
