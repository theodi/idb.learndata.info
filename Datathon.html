datathon

Definition: datathons are high energy, marathon-style events where mission-driven organizations work alongside teams of volunteer data scientists, developers, and designers to use data to gain insight into their programs, the communities they serve and more. All in 48 hours or less.[1]

General Objectives:

  • To provide NGOs with data science capacity and capability so that they can extract full value from their data and solve social, environmental, and community problems.
  • To derive novel and interesting insight from the data of participating NGOs and non-profit organisations.
  • To improve the quality and consistency of the information with which the NGO works.

Audience: Non Government Organisations (NGOs), data scientists, analysts, researchers, academics.

Method: Exploration with the target organisation to scope and frame question, preparation of data, weekend event, presentation of findings/analysis/outputs, integration of findings into everyday work.

Note: “There are weeks of work ahead (of the datathon) to scope the projects and prep the data followed by months of impact that ripple out after the weekend is over.“[2]

Types of datathon:

  • Humanitarian/social cause
  • Dataset accuracy/integrity
  • Organisational investment - community engagement, recruitment, innovation, capability

Topic: Mission or task is set by a single organisation and could be driven by social/humanitarian need or by a specific dataset.

Participant time commitment: 1-2 days (could participate for few hours only)

Duration of datathon: 1-2 days

Outputs:

  • Answers to pressing questions encountered by the subject organisation
  • Visualisations, analyses, graphs or new datasets

Outcomes:

  • New outlook/perspective on the power of data - datathons offer a powerful introduction to data science for all organisations involved
  • Clearer, common, understanding of how data science can be used to advance their missions
  • Refreshed perspective on the data and its value/use
  • Identify talented participants to fulfil vacant roles

Case Studies

1. DataKind, Washington DC, May 2016

Format: Exploration to scope data and understand data owners key questions, prepare and clean data, 2 day datathon event which culminates in ‘hand off’ when deliverables are handed over to the data ambassador from the NGO

Topic: Three non profit organisations present a challenge per datathon e.g. American Red Cross - targeting efforts to prevent home fires across the U.S. and Capital Area Food Bank - providing better nutrition to DC-area families in need

Incentive: Sense of achievement/contribution and philanthropy, professional development.

Goal: To help four local DC non-profits use data science to amplify their impact.

2. Data without Borders, New York City, 2011 - became DataKind in April 2012

Format: Match non-profits in need of data analysis with freelance and pro bono data scientists who can work to help them with data collection, analysis, visualisation, or decision support.

Topic: Determined by NGO

Incentive: Personal satisfaction and professional development, philanthropy.

Goal: To provide NGOs with data science capacity and capability so that they can extract full value from their data and solve social, environmental, and community problems. To derive novel and interesting insight from the data of participating NGO's and non-profits.

Big companies like Google and Amazon recognize the importance of dedicated data science teams and can support full time analyst.Non-profits, though they may have rich and interesting datasets, don't have the resources to capitalise on their data or may not even know the value of the data they already collect.

Data Without Borders aims to close that gap through a data scientist exchange, bringing exciting new problems to the data community and helping to solve social, environmental, and community problems alongside non-profits and NGOs.

3. Ann Arbor datathon, A2datathon, University of Michigan Campus

Format: Daylong event (08:30 - 21:00) which is open and free to students and community members. Stories and datasets for local non-profits are provided and the attendees take on the rest. datathonrs are free to collaborate and work on the data in whatever way they choose.

Topic: Set by participating non-profits - could be a dataset they want to explore or a specific challenge e.g. “explore links between food insecurity as well as job insecurity/skills and literacy using the National Longitudinal Survey”

Incentive: Peer learning, contribute to a social cause,

Goal: To help people of all experience levels feel empowered to use data — and have fun while doing it.

To connect experts and professionals with students and novices, so that both groups learn together (any may specialise in data collection, analysis and presentation)

4. Kraft datathon, Kraft, Chicago, 2015 (Partners: Intel, SAP and IBM)

Format: 20 teams of 3-5 college students who pre-register to attend 2 day event with assigned problem statement and coach

Topic: Use Kraft’s customer database (consumer shopping behaviour and habits) to spot the next big trend or uncover a never-before-seen pattern of consumer behavior.

Incentive: Three teams will win $5000 cash prize and extraordinary access to its proprietary database containing decades of consumer intelligence. Opportunity to learn from and present to senior executives in marketing and information technology.

Goal: To leverage technology and ingenuity to solve real-world challenges outside the classroom or tech lab. Students will look for new connections and patterns within the data and find ways to bring those insights to life. Kraft seeks new thinking and great ideas to help drive their business. Recruitment opportunity for Kraft.

Judging criteria: Creative Thinking, Critical Thinking, Problem Solving, Communication/Verbal Presentation and Teamwork.
